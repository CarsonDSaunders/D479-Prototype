About Taniti

Nestled in the heart of the Pacific, Taniti is a hidden tropical gem waiting to be discovered. This small island, under 500 square miles in size, boasts stunning landscapes ranging from pristine white sandy beaches to rugged, rocky shores. Its lush rainforests are home to vibrant wildlife, while its mountainous interior, dominated by an active volcano, offers adventure for the more daring traveler. The island’s welcoming indigenous population of 20,000 has a rich cultural history, and its local economy, once reliant on fishing and agriculture, has flourished with the rise of tourism. Whether you're exploring the quaint architecture of Taniti City or hiking through verdant rainforests, Taniti promises both relaxation and excitement.

Taniti offers a variety of activities for visitors to enjoy. Spend your days lounging by the beach or snorkeling in the crystal-clear waters of Yellow Leaf Bay, then head to one of the island's 10 diverse restaurants for fresh seafood or international cuisine. From helicopter rides and zip-lining through the rainforest to visiting the local history museum and art galleries, there's something for every traveler. With a range of accommodations — from cozy bed-and-breakfasts to a four-star resort — and a well-connected transportation system, including buses, taxis, and bike rentals, getting around Taniti is both easy and enjoyable. Whether you're here to unwind or explore, Taniti offers an unforgettable experience for all.
