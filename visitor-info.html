Visitor Information

Q: How do I get to Taniti?

A: Most visitors arrive by air. Taniti has a small airport that accommodates small jets and propeller planes. Expansion is underway to allow larger jets to land in the future. There is also a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay for one night per week.

Q: What transportation options are available on the island?

A: Public buses run daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. in Taniti City, while private buses serve the rest of the island. Taxis are available in Taniti City, and you can rent cars from a local rental agency near the airport. Bicycles and helmets are also available to rent.

Q: What is the currency used in Taniti?

A: Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its official currency. However, many businesses also accept euros and yen. Currency exchange services are available at local banks, and major credit cards are widely accepted.

Q: Is English spoken on Taniti?

A: While many younger residents, especially in Taniti City, speak fluent English, older residents and those in rural areas may not. It’s helpful to learn a few basic phrases in the local language for your visit.

Q: What is the power outlet type on Taniti?

A: Power outlets in Taniti supply 120 volts, the same as in the United States. Visitors from countries with different voltage systems should bring appropriate adapters.

Q: Is Taniti a safe place to visit?

A: Taniti is generally very safe, with violent crime being rare. However, as tourism increases, pickpocketing and petty crimes have been reported, so visitors are advised to take basic precautions.

Q: Are there any restrictions on alcohol consumption?

A: Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9 a.m. The drinking age is 18, though it is not strictly enforced.

Q: What medical facilities are available on Taniti?

A: There is one hospital on the island, which has multilingual staff, and several smaller clinics to accommodate visitors in need of medical care.

Q: Are there any national holidays that might affect my visit?

A: Taniti observes many national holidays, during which tourist attractions and restaurants may be closed. It’s advisable to check the local calendar and plan accordingly.
